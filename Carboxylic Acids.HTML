Aldehydes, Ketones, and Carboxylic Acids - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz - Carboxylic Acids

1. Carboxylic acids are organic compounds containing a __________ functional group ($COOH$).
carboxyl
2. The general formula of aliphatic monocarboxylic acids is $RCOOH$. The carboxyl carbon atom is __________ hybridized and has a trigonal planar geometry.
$sp^2$
3. The IUPAC nomenclature of carboxylic acids involves replacing the '-e' of the corresponding alkane with '-__________ acid'. The carboxyl carbon is always numbered as 1.
oic
4. Carboxylic acids have considerably __________ boiling points than the corresponding alcohols, ketones, and aldehydes of similar molecular mass due to stronger intermolecular hydrogen bonding resulting from the presence of two electronegative oxygen atoms.
higher
5. Lower carboxylic acids are soluble in water due to the formation of __________ bonds with water molecules. Solubility decreases with increasing molecular mass.
hydrogen
6. Carboxylic acids are __________ acids than phenols and alcohols due to the greater resonance stabilization of the carboxylate anion.
stronger
7. The acidity of carboxylic acids is increased by electron-__________ groups (like $-NO_2, -CN, -X$) attached to the alkyl or aryl group.
withdrawing
8. The acidity of carboxylic acids is decreased by electron-__________ groups (like $-CH_3, -OR$) attached to the alkyl or aryl group.
donating
9. Carboxylic acids react with metals to form salts and __________ gas.
hydrogen ($H_2$)
10. Carboxylic acids react with bases (like $NaOH, KOH$) to form __________ and water.
salts
11. Carboxylic acids react with alcohols in the presence of an acid catalyst (like concentrated $H_2SO_4$ or $HCl$) to form esters and water. This reaction is called __________.
esterification
12. Carboxylic acids react with thionyl chloride ($SOCl_2$), phosphorus pentachloride ($PCl_5$), or phosphorus trichloride ($PCl_3$) to form acyl __________ (acid chlorides).
chlorides
13. Carboxylic acids react with ammonia to form ammonium salts, which on heating lose water to form __________.
amides
14. Reduction of carboxylic acids with lithium aluminum hydride ($LiAlH_4$) yields primary __________. Sodium borohydride ($NaBH_4$) does not usually reduce carboxylic acids.
alcohols
15. Decarboxylation of carboxylic acids involves the removal of carbon dioxide ($CO_2$) to form __________. This can be achieved by heating the sodium salt of the carboxylic acid with soda lime ($NaOH$ and $CaO$).
alkanes
16. Formic acid ($HCOOH$) is the simplest carboxylic acid and has a __________ odor.
pungent
17. Acetic acid ($CH_3COOH$) is commonly known as glacial acetic acid in its anhydrous form and is the main component of __________.
vinegar
18. Benzoic acid ($C_6H_5COOH$) is an aromatic carboxylic acid and is used as a food __________.
preservative
19. Dicarboxylic acids contain __________ carboxyl groups. Examples include oxalic acid, malonic acid, succinic acid, glutaric acid, adipic acid, pimelic acid, azelaic acid, and sebacic acid.
two
20. Carboxylic acids can be prepared by the oxidation of primary alcohols and __________ with strong oxidizing agents like $KMnO_4$ or $K_2Cr_2O_7$ in acidic medium.
aldehydes
21. Hydrolysis of nitriles ($RCN$) in acidic or basic medium yields __________.
carboxylic acids
22. Grignard reagents ($RMgX$) react with carbon dioxide ($CO_2$) followed by acidification to yield __________. This is a method for increasing the number of carbon atoms in a chain.
carboxylic acids
23. Oxidation of alkylbenzenes with $KMnO_4$ or $K_2Cr_2O_7$ gives benzoic acid, regardless of the length of the alkyl chain, provided there is at least one hydrogen atom on the __________ carbon atom.
benzylic
24. The Hell-Volhard-Zelinsky reaction involves the $\alpha$-halogenation of carboxylic acids using $X_2$ ($Cl_2$ or $Br_2$) in the presence of red __________ followed by hydrolysis.
phosphorus
25. Aromatic carboxylic acids undergo electrophilic substitution reactions on the benzene ring. The $-COOH$ group is __________ and meta directing.
deactivating
