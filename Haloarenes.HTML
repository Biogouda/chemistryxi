Haloarenes - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

1. Haloarenes are organic compounds in which one or more hydrogen atoms of an __________ ring have been replaced by halogen atoms.
aromatic
2. The carbon-halogen bond in haloarenes has some __________ bond character due to resonance.
double
3. Haloarenes are generally __________ reactive towards nucleophilic substitution reactions compared to haloalkanes.
less
4. The reduced reactivity of haloarenes towards nucleophilic substitution is due to resonance stabilization, $sp^2$ hybridization of the carbon atom bearing the halogen, and possible __________ of the nucleophile by the electron-rich arene ring.
repulsion
5. The presence of electron-__________ groups (like $-NO_2$) at ortho and para positions to the halogen in haloarenes significantly facilitates nucleophilic substitution reactions.
withdrawing
6. The reaction of chlorobenzene with sodium hydroxide at high temperature and pressure to form phenol is known as __________ process.
Dow's
7. Haloarenes undergo electrophilic __________ reactions on the aromatic ring.
substitution
8. The halogen atom in haloarenes is __________ directing in electrophilic substitution reactions, although it is deactivating.
ortho- and para-
9. The reaction of haloarenes with sodium metal in dry ether to form diphenyl is known as __________ reaction.
Wurtz-Fittig
10. The reaction of aryl halides with copper powder heated in a sealed tube gives diaryl compounds. This reaction is called __________ reaction.
Ullmann
11. Chlorobenzene can be prepared by the reaction of benzene with chlorine in the presence of anhydrous aluminum chloride ($AlCl_3$). This is an example of electrophilic __________.
chlorination
12. The carbon-halogen bond length in chlorobenzene is __________ than that in chloromethane due to partial double bond character.
shorter
13. The dipole moment of chlorobenzene is __________ than that of chloromethane because the $sp^2$ hybridized carbon in chlorobenzene is more electronegative than the $sp^3$ hybridized carbon in chloromethane.
smaller
14. In the nucleophilic aromatic substitution ($S_NAr$) reaction involving haloarenes with electron-withdrawing groups, the reaction proceeds via a __________ intermediate.
Meisenheimer complex (carbanion)
15. The order of reactivity of halogens in electrophilic aromatic substitution of haloarenes is $F > Cl > Br > I$ due to the __________ electronegativity of fluorine.
highest
16. However, the overall activating-deactivating influence of halogens is deactivating due to their strong __________ inductive effect (-I) which outweighs their resonance effect (+R).
electron-withdrawing
17. The resonance effect (+R) of halogens in haloarenes leads to increased electron density at the __________ and para positions.
ortho-
18. Aryl halides are used as starting materials for the synthesis of various organic compounds through reactions like Friedel-Crafts reaction, but haloarenes themselves undergo Friedel-Crafts reaction __________ readily than benzene.
less
19. The reaction of bromobenzene with magnesium in dry ether gives __________.
phenylmagnesium bromide ($C_6H_5MgBr$)
20. Aryl halides can undergo coupling reactions with alkenes in the presence of palladium catalysts, known as __________ reactions.
Heck
21. The Sandmeyer reaction involves the reaction of diazonium salts (obtained from aromatic amines) with $CuCl/HCl$, $CuBr/HBr$, or $KI$ to yield __________.
aryl halides
22. The Gattermann reaction is similar to the Sandmeyer reaction but uses copper powder and $HCl$ or $HBr$ instead of cuprous halides to obtain __________ and bromobenzene.
chlorobenzene
23. Iodoarenes can be prepared by the direct iodination of arenes in the presence of an __________ agent like $HNO_3$ or $H_2SO_4$.
oxidizing
24. Fluoroarenes are commonly synthesized using the __________ reaction, which involves treating diazonium salts with fluoroboric acid ($HBF_4$) followed by heating.
Balz-Schiemann
25. Polyhalogenated aromatic compounds like hexachlorobenzene are known for their __________ and environmental persistence.
toxicity
