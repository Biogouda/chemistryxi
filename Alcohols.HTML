Alcohols, Phenols, and Ethers - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

Alcohols

1. Alcohols are organic compounds containing one or more __________ groups attached to an alkyl group.
hydroxyl (-OH)
2. Alcohols are classified as primary, secondary, or tertiary depending on the number of alkyl groups attached to the carbon atom bearing the __________ group.
hydroxyl
3. The general formula of monohydric saturated alcohols is $C_nH_{2n+1}OH$, which can also be written as $C_nH_{2n+2}O$. They are isomeric with __________.
ethers
4. The boiling points of alcohols are considerably __________ than those of the corresponding alkanes and ethers due to intermolecular hydrogen bonding.
higher
5. The solubility of lower alcohols in water is high due to the formation of __________ bonds with water molecules.
hydrogen
6. Alcohols react with sodium metal to form __________ and hydrogen gas, indicating their acidic nature.
alkoxides
7. The acidity of alcohols follows the order: primary > secondary > __________.
tertiary
8. Alcohols undergo dehydration in the presence of concentrated sulfuric acid or phosphoric acid to form alkenes or __________.
ethers
9. Oxidation of primary alcohols with strong oxidizing agents like $KMnO_4$ or $K_2Cr_2O_7$ yields carboxylic acids, while milder oxidation can yield __________.
aldehydes
10. Oxidation of secondary alcohols yields __________.
ketones
11. Tertiary alcohols do not undergo oxidation easily because they lack a hydrogen atom on the carbon atom bearing the __________ group.
hydroxyl
12. The reaction of alcohols with carboxylic acids in the presence of an acid catalyst to form esters and water is called __________ reaction.
esterification
13. Alcohols react with phosphorus halides ($PCl_5, PCl_3, PBr_3$) or thionyl chloride ($SOCl_2$) to form __________.
haloalkanes (alkyl halides)
14. Ethanol ($CH_3CH_2OH$) is produced on a large scale by the fermentation of sugars or by the hydration of __________.
ethene (ethylene)
15. Methanol ($CH_3OH$) is produced industrially by the catalytic hydrogenation of carbon monoxide at high temperature and __________.
pressure
16. The Lucas test is used to distinguish between primary, secondary, and tertiary alcohols based on their reactivity with Lucas reagent, which is anhydrous $ZnCl_2$ in concentrated __________.
HCl
17. Tertiary alcohols react __________ with Lucas reagent to give a cloudy solution immediately.
fastest
18. Secondary alcohols react with Lucas reagent to give a cloudy solution within __________ minutes.
5-10
19. Primary alcohols do not react appreciably with Lucas reagent at __________ temperature.
room
20. Vicinal diols have hydroxyl groups on __________ adjacent carbon atoms.
two
21. Geminal diols have two hydroxyl groups on the __________ carbon atom and are generally unstable, losing a water molecule to form a carbonyl compound.
same
22. Glycerol is a trihydric alcohol with the IUPAC name propane-1,2,3-__________.
triol
23. The dehydration of alcohols to form alkenes follows __________ rule, where the more substituted alkene is the major product.
Saytzeff's
24. Alcohols can be converted to ethers by Williamson __________ synthesis.
ether
25. The $-\text{OH}$ group in alcohols is directly attached to an $sp^3$ hybridized carbon atom. In phenols, the $-\text{OH}$ group is directly attached to an $sp^2$ hybridized carbon atom of the __________ ring.
benzene
