Group 18 Elements - The p-Block Elements - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

1. The elements belonging to Group 18 of the periodic table are collectively known as __________ gases or noble gases.
inert
2. The general electronic configuration of Group 18 elements is $ns^2 np^?$ (except for Helium).
6
3. The first element of Group 18 is __________.
helium (He)
4. Noble gases have very __________ ionization enthalpies due to their stable electronic configurations.
high
5. Noble gases have __________ electron affinities.
very low or almost zero
6. The boiling points of noble gases are very __________ due to weak London dispersion forces.
low
7. The first noble gas compound was prepared by Neil Bartlett in 1962 and involved the reaction of xenon with __________.
platinum hexafluoride ($PtF_6$)
8. Xenon forms a number of compounds, mainly with highly electronegative elements like fluorine and __________.
oxygen
9. The hybridization of xenon in $XeF_2$ is __________.
$sp^3d$
10. The shape of $XeF_2$ molecule is __________.
linear
11. The hybridization of xenon in $XeF_4$ is __________.
$sp^3d^2$
12. The shape of $XeF_4$ molecule is __________.
square planar
13. The hybridization of xenon in $XeF_6$ is __________.
$sp^3d^3$
14. The shape of $XeF_6$ molecule is __________.
distorted octahedral or capped octahedron
15. Xenon trioxide ($XeO_3$) has a __________ shape.
pyramidal
16. Xenon oxytetrafluoride ($XeOF_4$) has a __________ shape.
square pyramidal
17. Helium is used in filling balloons for meteorological observations because it is light and __________.
non-inflammable
18. Liquid helium is used as a cryogenic agent for carrying out experiments at __________ temperatures.
very low
19. Neon is commonly used in __________ signs.
advertising
20. Argon is used to provide an inert atmosphere in welding of metals and for filling electric __________.
bulbs
21. Radon is a radioactive noble gas and is used in the treatment of __________.
cancer
22. The tendency of noble gases to form compounds __________ down the group due to decreasing ionization enthalpy.
increases
23. Helium has only two electrons and its electronic configuration is $1s^2$. It resembles Group 2 elements in having a filled s-orbital but is placed in Group 18 because of its __________ electronic configuration.
stable (duplet)
24. Krypton and xenon are known to form some compounds, mainly with fluorine and oxygen, but __________ has not yet formed any well-characterized compounds.
argon
25. The noble gases are monoatomic because they have __________ intermolecular forces.
very weak
