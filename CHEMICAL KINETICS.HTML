Chemical Kinetics - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

1. Chemical kinetics is the branch of chemistry that deals with the __________ of chemical reactions and the factors affecting them.
rate
2. The rate of a chemical reaction is the change in the __________ of a reactant or a product per unit time.
concentration
3. For a reaction $aA + bB \rightarrow cC + dD$, the rate of reaction can be expressed as $-\frac{1}{a}\frac{d[A]}{dt} = -\frac{1}{b}\frac{d[B]}{dt} = +\frac{1}{c}\frac{d[C]}{dt} = +\frac{1}{d}\frac{d[D]}{dt}$. The negative sign indicates the __________ of reactants.
disappearance
4. The rate law expresses the rate of a reaction in terms of the __________ of the reactants raised to some powers.
concentrations
5. For a reaction $aA + bB \rightarrow products$, if the experimentally determined rate law is rate $= k[A]^x[B]^y$, then $x$ is the order of the reaction with respect to A, and $y$ is the order with respect to B. The overall order of the reaction is __________.
x + y
6. The sum of the powers of the concentration of the reactants in the rate law expression is called the __________ of the reaction.
order
7. A reaction whose rate is independent of the concentration of the reactant is called a __________ order reaction.
zero
8. The rate constant ($k$) of a reaction is a proportionality constant that relates the rate of the reaction to the __________ of the reactants.
concentrations
9. The integrated rate law for a zero-order reaction ($A \rightarrow products$) is $[A]_t = [A]_0 - __________.
kt
10. The integrated rate law for a first-order reaction ($A \rightarrow products$) is $\ln[A]_t = \ln[A]_0 - __________.
kt
11. The time required for half of the reactant to be converted into product is called the __________ of the reaction.
half-life
12. The half-life of a zero-order reaction is directly proportional to the initial concentration of the __________.
reactant
13. The half-life of a first-order reaction is __________ of the initial concentration of the reactant.
independent
14. The rate of a reaction generally __________ with an increase in temperature.
increases
15. According to the collision theory, the rate of a reaction depends on the frequency of effective __________.
collisions
16. For a collision to be effective, the colliding molecules must possess sufficient __________ energy and proper orientation.
kinetic
17. The minimum energy that the reactant molecules must possess for a collision to result in a chemical reaction is called the __________ energy ($E_a$).
activation
18. The Arrhenius equation relates the rate constant ($k$) of a reaction to the activation energy ($E_a$) and temperature (T): $k = A e^{-E_a/RT}$, where A is the __________ factor.
frequency or pre-exponential
19. A __________ is a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent chemical change.
catalyst
20. A catalyst provides an alternative reaction pathway with a __________ activation energy.
lower
21. Catalysts that are in the same phase as the reactants are called __________ catalysts.
homogeneous
22. Catalysts that are in a different phase from the reactants are called __________ catalysts.
heterogeneous
23. Enzymes are biological catalysts that are generally __________ in nature.
proteins
24. The mechanism of a reaction describes the sequence of elementary steps that make up the overall __________.
reaction
25. The slowest step in a reaction mechanism is called the __________ step, which determines the rate of the overall reaction.
rate-determining
