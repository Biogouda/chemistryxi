Chemicals in Medicines - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

Antacids

1. __________ are chemical substances that neutralize excess acid in the stomach and raise the pH to an appropriate level.
Antacids
2. Common examples of antacids include magnesium hydroxide ($Mg(OH)_2$), aluminum hydroxide ($Al(OH)_3$), and sodium bicarbonate ($NaHCO_3$). Another effective antacid is __________.
cimetidine or ranitidine (H2 receptor antagonists)
3. Cimetidine and ranitidine work by inhibiting the secretion of __________ in the stomach.
hydrochloric acid (HCl)

Antihistamines

4. __________ are drugs that counteract the effects of histamine, which is released in the body during allergic reactions.
Antihistamines
5. Histamine stimulates the secretion of acid in the stomach and causes __________ of smooth muscles in the bronchi and gut.
contraction
6. Older antihistamines like diphenhydramine and brompheniramine often cause __________. Newer antihistamines like loratadine and cetirizine are designed to minimize this side effect.
drowsiness (sedation)

Analgesics

7. __________ are drugs that relieve pain. They are classified as non-narcotic (non-addictive) and narcotic (addictive).
Analgesics
8. Aspirin (acetylsalicylic acid) and paracetamol (acetaminophen) are examples of __________ analgesics. They also have antipyretic (fever-reducing) properties.
non-narcotic
9. Morphine and codeine are examples of __________ analgesics, which are often used for severe pain relief but can be addictive.
narcotic

Tranquilizers

10. __________ are drugs that reduce anxiety and tension, often used to treat mental disorders.
Tranquilizers
11. Minor tranquilizers, such as diazepam and alprazolam (benzodiazepines), are used to relieve __________ and anxiety.
stress
12. Major tranquilizers or neuroleptics, such as haloperidol and chlorpromazine, are used to treat severe mental disorders like __________.
psychosis and schizophrenia

Antimicrobials

13. __________ are drugs that kill or inhibit the growth of microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites.
Antimicrobials

Antibiotics

14. __________ are a type of antimicrobial specifically effective against bacterial infections. Penicillin was one of the first discovered.
Antibiotics
15. Broad-spectrum antibiotics are effective against a wide range of bacteria, while narrow-spectrum antibiotics are effective against a __________ group of bacteria.
limited
16. Examples of antibiotics include penicillin, amoxicillin, tetracycline, and __________.
erythromycin

Antivirals

17. __________ are drugs used to treat viral infections. They often work by inhibiting viral replication.
Antivirals
18. Examples of antiviral drugs include acyclovir (for herpes) and __________ (for HIV).
zidovudine (AZT)

Antifungals

19. __________ are drugs used to treat fungal infections.
Antifungals
20. Examples of antifungal drugs include fluconazole and __________.
amphotericin B

Antiparasitics

21. __________ are drugs used to treat parasitic infections, such as malaria and worm infestations.
Antiparasitics
22. Examples of antiparasitic drugs include quinine (for malaria) and __________ (for worm infections).
mebendazole

Antifertility Drugs

23. __________ are chemical substances used to prevent conception (pregnancy).
Antifertility drugs
24. Oral contraceptives often contain synthetic __________ and progestin hormones, which prevent ovulation.
estrogen
25. Norethindrone and ethinylestradiol are examples of __________ used in oral contraceptives.
synthetic hormones
