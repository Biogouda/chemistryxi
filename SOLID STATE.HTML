Solid State Chemistry Quiz

1. Solids with a regular arrangement of constituent particles are called __________ solids.
crystalline
2. Solids lacking a long-range order of constituent particles are called __________ solids.
amorphous
3. The smallest repeating unit in a crystal lattice is called a __________.
unit cell
4. The number of atoms per unit cell in a simple cubic lattice is __________.
1
5. The number of atoms per unit cell in a body-centered cubic lattice is __________.
2
6. The number of atoms per unit cell in a face-centered cubic lattice is __________.
4
7. The fraction of the total volume occupied by the atoms in a simple cubic unit cell is __________.
$\frac{\pi}{6}$ or approximately 0.52
8. The fraction of the total volume occupied by the atoms in a body-centered cubic unit cell is __________.
$\frac{\sqrt{3}\pi}{8}$ or approximately 0.68
9. The fraction of the total volume occupied by the atoms in a face-centered cubic unit cell is __________.
$\frac{\sqrt{2}\pi}{6}$ or approximately 0.74
10. The coordination number of each sphere in a simple cubic packing is __________.
6
11. The coordination number of each sphere in a body-centered cubic packing is __________.
8
12. The coordination number of each sphere in a face-centered cubic packing is __________.
12
13. The most efficient packing of spheres in two dimensions is __________ packing.
hexagonal close
14. The two types of voids found in a close-packed structure are __________ and __________ voids.
tetrahedral, octahedral
15. The coordination number of a tetrahedral void is __________.
4
16. The coordination number of an octahedral void is __________.
6
17. If R is the radius of the anion and r is the radius of the cation, the radius ratio (r/R) for a tetrahedral void is in the range of __________.
0.225 - 0.414
18. If R is the radius of the anion and r is the radius of the cation, the radius ratio (r/R) for an octahedral void is in the range of __________.
0.414 - 0.732
19. A solid in which constituent particles are held together by metallic bonds is called a __________ solid.
metallic
20. A solid in which constituent particles are held together by ionic bonds is called an __________ solid.
ionic
21. A solid in which constituent particles are held together by covalent bonds is called a __________ solid.
covalent or network
22. A solid in which constituent particles are held together by weak van der Waals forces is called a __________ solid.
molecular
23. Schottky defects arise due to the absence of __________ and __________ from their lattice sites.
cations, anions
24. Frenkel defects arise when an ion leaves its lattice site and occupies an __________ site.
interstitial
25. Schottky defects are commonly observed in ionic compounds with a small difference in the size of __________ and __________.
cation, anion
26. Frenkel defects are commonly observed in ionic compounds with a large difference in the size of __________ and __________.
cation, anion
27. Metal deficiency defects can arise due to the absence of __________ or the presence of extra __________ occupying interstitial sites.
metal cations, extra anions
28. Metal excess defects can arise due to anionic __________ or the presence of extra __________ occupying interstitial sites.
vacancies, extra cations
29. F-centers are anionic vacancies occupied by __________.
electrons
30. Substances that show magnetism due to unpaired electrons are called __________ substances.
paramagnetic
31. Substances that are weakly repelled by a magnetic field are called __________ substances.
diamagnetic
32. Substances that are weakly attracted by a magnetic field are called __________ substances.
paramagnetic
33. Substances that show permanent magnetism even in the absence of a magnetic field are called __________ substances.
ferromagnetic
34. In ferromagnetic substances, the magnetic domains are __________ oriented.
spontaneously and parallelly
35. In antiferromagnetic substances, the magnetic domains are aligned in __________ directions and cancel out each other's magnetic moment.
opposite
36. In ferrimagnetic substances, the magnetic domains are aligned in __________ directions with unequal magnetic moments, resulting in a net magnetic moment.
parallel and anti-parallel
37. The temperature above which a ferromagnetic substance becomes paramagnetic is called the __________ temperature.
Curie
38. The temperature below which an antiferromagnetic substance becomes paramagnetic is called the __________ temperature or Néel temperature.
Néel
39. Solids that can conduct electricity are called __________.
conductors
40. Solids that do not conduct electricity are called __________.
insulators
41. Solids with conductivity between that of conductors and insulators are called __________.
semiconductors
42. In metals, the conductivity __________ with an increase in temperature.
decreases
43. In semiconductors, the conductivity generally __________ with an increase in temperature.
increases
44. Semiconductors whose conductivity is due to the presence of impurities are called __________ semiconductors.
extrinsic
45. Semiconductors in which the increased conductivity is due to their intrinsic electronic structure are called __________ semiconductors.
intrinsic
46. An n-type semiconductor is formed when silicon is doped with a __________ group element.
group 15
47. A p-type semiconductor is formed when silicon is doped with a __________ group element.
group 13
48. In an n-type semiconductor, the majority charge carriers are __________.
electrons
49. In a p-type semiconductor, the majority charge carriers are __________.
holes
50. A p-n junction diode allows current to flow easily in the __________ bias.
forward
51. A __________ cell converts solar energy directly into electrical energy.
photovoltaic or solar
52. Certain solids exhibit the property of generating electricity when subjected to mechanical stress or pressure; this phenomenon is called __________.
piezoelectricity
53. Some crystalline solids exhibit different physical properties in different directions; this phenomenon is called __________.
anisotropy
