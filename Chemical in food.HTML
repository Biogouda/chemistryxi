Chemicals in Food - Preservatives, Sweeteners, Antioxidants

Food Preservatives

1. __________ are chemical substances added to food to prevent spoilage caused by microbial growth (bacteria, yeasts, molds) and enzymatic action, thereby prolonging their shelf life.
Food preservatives
2. Common natural preservatives include __________ (used in jams and jellies), salt (used in pickles and cured meats), and vinegar (acetic acid, used in pickling).
sugar
3. Examples of chemical preservatives include sodium benzoate, potassium sorbate, and __________ (used in processed meats).
sulfur dioxide and sulfites
4. Benzoates and sorbates work by inhibiting the growth of __________ and molds.
yeasts
5. Sulfur dioxide and sulfites act as preservatives and also prevent __________ in some foods.
browning

Artificial Sweetening Agents

6. __________ are chemical substances that provide a sweet taste to food and beverages but have significantly fewer calories than natural sugars like sucrose.
Artificial sweetening agents
7. Saccharin was one of the first artificial sweeteners discovered. It is much __________ than sucrose.
sweeter (around 550 times)
8. Aspartame is an artificial sweetener composed of two amino acids: aspartic acid and __________. It is approximately 100-200 times sweeter than sucrose.
phenylalanine
9. Sucralose is a trichlorinated derivative of sucrose and is about __________ times sweeter than sucrose. It is stable at high temperatures.
600
10. Alitame is another artificial sweetener that is even sweeter than aspartame, approximately __________ times sweeter than sucrose.
2000
11. Cyclamates are artificial sweeteners that are about __________ times sweeter than sucrose. Their use is restricted in some countries due to safety concerns.
30

Antioxidants

12. __________ are substances that prevent or slow down oxidation of food components, thereby preventing rancidity in fats and oils and discoloration in fruits and vegetables.
Antioxidants
13. Oxidation in food can lead to the development of unpleasant odors and flavors, and also the loss of nutritional value. This process often involves the formation of __________ radicals.
free
14. Natural antioxidants found in foods include Vitamin C (ascorbic acid), Vitamin E (tocopherols), and __________.
beta-carotene
15. Synthetic antioxidants commonly used in food include butylated hydroxyanisole (BHA) and butylated hydroxytoluene (__________).
BHT
16. Antioxidants work by scavenging __________ radicals, thus inhibiting the chain reactions of oxidation.
free
17. The use of antioxidants helps to maintain the quality, flavor, color, and nutritional value of processed foods.
true
