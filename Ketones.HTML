Aldehydes, Ketones, and Carboxylic Acids - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz - Ketones

1. Ketones are organic compounds containing a carbonyl group ($C=O$) with the carbon atom bonded to two __________ groups or aryl groups.
alkyl (R)
2. The general formula of aliphatic ketones is $RCOR'$, where $R$ and $R'$ can be the same or different alkyl groups. The carbonyl group is located __________ within the carbon chain.
within
3. The IUPAC nomenclature of ketones involves replacing the '-e' of the corresponding alkane with '-__________'. The position of the carbonyl group is indicated by a number.
one
4. The carbon atom of the carbonyl group in ketones is __________ hybridized and has a trigonal planar geometry.
$sp^2$
5. Ketones have __________ boiling points than the corresponding alkanes but __________ boiling points than the corresponding alcohols of similar molecular mass.
higher; lower
6. Lower ketones are soluble in water due to the formation of hydrogen bonds with water molecules through the __________ oxygen atom.
carbonyl
7. Ketones undergo nucleophilic __________ reactions at the electrophilic carbonyl carbon, similar to aldehydes.
addition
8. Ketones react with alcohols in the presence of dry $HCl$ to form hemiketals and further reaction yields __________.
ketals
9. Ketones react with ammonia derivatives to form condensation products such as oximes, hydrazones, and __________.
semicarbazones
10. Ketones can be reduced to secondary alcohols using reducing agents like $LiAlH_4$ or $NaBH_4$. This reduction involves the addition of __________ across the carbonyl double bond.
hydrogen
11. Oxidation of ketones requires vigorous conditions and strong oxidizing agents, leading to the cleavage of $C-C$ bonds and formation of __________ with fewer carbon atoms.
carboxylic acids
12. Ketones do not reduce __________ reagent, which distinguishes them from aldehydes.
Tollens'
13. Ketones do not reduce __________ solution, which further distinguishes them from aldehydes.
Fehling's
14. Ketones having $\alpha$-hydrogen atoms undergo aldol condensation in the presence of dilute alkali to form $\beta$-hydroxy ketones (ketols), which can further dehydrate to $\alpha,\beta$-unsaturated __________.
ketones
15. Ketones do not undergo Cannizzaro reaction because they have $\alpha$-__________ atoms.
hydrogen
16. Acetone ($CH_3COCH_3$) is the simplest ketone and is a widely used __________ in laboratories and industries.
solvent
17. Methyl ethyl ketone (MEK) or butan-2-one ($CH_3COCH_2CH_3$) is also an important industrial __________.
solvent
18. Aromatic ketones, like acetophenone ($C_6H_5COCH_3$), undergo electrophilic substitution reactions on the benzene ring. The $-COR$ group is __________ and meta directing.
deactivating
19. Secondary alcohols can be oxidized to ketones using oxidizing agents like $K_2Cr_2O_7$ or $CrO_3$. Pyridinium chlorochromate (PCC) can also be used for this purpose.
true
20. Friedel-Crafts acylation involves the reaction of benzene or substituted benzenes with acyl chlorides or anhydrides in the presence of anhydrous $AlCl_3$ to yield aromatic __________.
ketones
21. Grignard reagents react with acyl chlorides to form tertiary alcohols after hydrolysis. However, reaction with nitriles followed by hydrolysis yields __________.
ketones
22. The oxidation of secondary alcohols with sodium dichromate in sulfuric acid typically yields __________.
ketones
23. Ketones undergo Wittig reaction with phosphorus ylides (phosphoranes) to form __________.
alkenes
24. The Clemmensen reduction (using $Zn-Hg/HCl$) and Wolff-Kishner reduction (using $NH_2NH_2/KOH$) are used for the reduction of ketones to __________.
alkanes
25. The haloform reaction is given by ketones containing at least one methyl group attached to the carbonyl carbon ($CH_3CO-$). Reaction with $X_2/NaOH$ ($X = Cl, Br, I$) yields a haloform ($CHX_3$) and a salt of a __________ acid.
carboxylic
