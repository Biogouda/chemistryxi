Polymers - Classification, Polymerization, Important Polymers

Classification of Polymers

1. Polymers are large molecules (macromolecules) composed of repeating structural units called __________, connected by covalent chemical bonds.
monomers
2. Based on their origin, polymers are classified into two main categories: __________ polymers and synthetic polymers.
natural
3. __________ polymers are obtained from natural sources, such as plants and animals. Examples include cellulose, starch, proteins, and natural rubber.
Natural
4. __________ polymers are man-made polymers synthesized in laboratories and industries. Examples include polythene, nylon, polyesters, and Bakelite.
Synthetic

Methods of Polymerization

Addition Polymerization

5. Addition polymerization involves the joining of monomer units without the loss of any atoms. This type of polymerization usually occurs with monomers containing __________ bonds (typically double or triple bonds).
unsaturated
6. Polythene (polyethylene) is a common example of an __________ polymer formed by the addition polymerization of ethene (ethylene).
addition

Condensation Polymerization

7. Condensation polymerization involves the joining of monomer units with the loss of small molecules such as water, alcohol, or carbon dioxide. Monomers typically contain __________ functional groups.
reactive (e.g., hydroxyl, carboxyl, amino)
8. Nylon is a synthetic polyamide produced by __________ polymerization between diamines and dicarboxylic acids.
condensation
9. Polyesters, such as Terylene (Dacron), are formed by the condensation reaction between dicarboxylic acids and __________.
diols (glycols)
10. Bakelite is a thermosetting polymer formed by the condensation reaction between phenol and __________.
formaldehyde

Copolymerization

11. Copolymerization is a polymerization reaction in which two or more different monomer species are allowed to polymerize together to form a polymer called a __________.
copolymer
12. Buna-S rubber is a copolymer of 1,3-butadiene and __________.
styrene

Some Important Polymers

Natural Polymers

  • Natural Rubber: A polymer of isoprene, known for its elasticity.
  • Cellulose: A polymer of glucose, the main structural component of plant cell walls.
  • Starch: A polymer of glucose, used as a storage polysaccharide in plants.
  • Proteins: Polymers of amino acids, essential for various biological functions.

Synthetic Polymers

  • Polythene (Polyethylene): A simple addition polymer of ethene, widely used in packaging and films.
  • Nylon: A synthetic polyamide with high tensile strength, used in fibers and plastics.
  • Polyester (e.g., Terylene/Dacron): Condensation polymers used in fabrics and bottles.
  • Bakelite: A thermosetting polymer used in electrical switches and handles.
  • Rubber (Synthetic): Various copolymers like Buna-S and Neoprene, with properties similar to or superior to natural rubber.

Biodegradable and Non-Biodegradable Polymers

13. __________ polymers can be broken down by microorganisms or natural processes into harmless substances. Examples include cellulose, starch, and some synthetic polymers like poly(lactic acid) (PLA) and poly($\beta$-hydroxybutyrate) (PHB).
Biodegradable
14. __________ polymers cannot be easily broken down by natural processes and persist in the environment, leading to pollution. Examples include polythene, nylon, polyesters, and PVC.
Non-biodegradable
15. The accumulation of __________ polymers in the environment poses a significant threat to ecosystems and human health.
non-biodegradable
