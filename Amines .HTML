Amines - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

1. Amines are organic compounds derived from ammonia ($NH_3$) by the replacement of one, two, or all three hydrogen atoms by __________ or aryl groups.
alkyl
2. Amines are classified as primary ($1^\circ$), secondary ($2^\circ$), or tertiary ($3^\circ$) based on the number of alkyl or aryl groups attached to the __________ atom.
nitrogen
3. The nitrogen atom in amines has one lone pair of electrons and is __________ hybridized, resulting in a pyramidal geometry.
$sp^3$
4. Amines have __________ boiling points than alkanes of similar molecular mass due to intermolecular hydrogen bonding (except for tertiary amines). However, their boiling points are __________ than those of alcohols due to weaker hydrogen bonding.
higher; lower
5. Lower aliphatic amines are soluble in water due to the formation of __________ bonds with water molecules. Solubility decreases with increasing molecular mass.
hydrogen
6. Amines are basic in nature due to the presence of a lone pair of electrons on the nitrogen atom, which they can donate to a __________ ion.
proton ($H^+$)
7. The basicity of amines depends on factors such as the availability of the lone pair of electrons, steric hindrance, and __________ of the conjugate acid.
stability
8. Aliphatic amines are generally __________ basic than ammonia. Electron-donating alkyl groups increase the electron density on the nitrogen atom.
more
9. Aromatic amines, like aniline, are __________ basic than aliphatic amines and ammonia due to the delocalization of the lone pair of electrons into the benzene ring through resonance.
less
10. Amines react with acids to form __________.
salts
11. Amines react with acyl chlorides and acid anhydrides to form __________ or substituted amides.
amides
12. Primary aliphatic amines react with nitrous acid ($HNO_2$) to form unstable diazonium salts, which decompose to give alcohols, nitrogen gas, and other products. Primary aromatic amines react with $HNO_2$ at low temperatures to form stable aromatic __________ salts.
diazonium
13. Secondary amines react with nitrous acid to form __________ compounds.
N-nitroso
14. Tertiary amines react with nitrous acid to form __________ salts.
nitrite
15. The reaction of primary amines with chloroform in the presence of alcoholic potassium hydroxide ($KOH$) is known as __________ test, which is used to detect the presence of primary amines. A foul-smelling isocyanide is formed.
carbylamine
16. Primary amines undergo alkylation on reaction with alkyl halides to form secondary and tertiary amines, and finally __________ salts.
quaternary ammonium
17. Reduction of nitro compounds ($RNO_2$ or $ArNO_2$) with metal/acid (like $Sn/HCl$ or $Fe/HCl$) or catalytic hydrogenation ($H_2/Ni, Pt, Pd$) yields primary __________.
amines
18. Reduction of amides ($RCONH_2$) with lithium aluminum hydride ($LiAlH_4$) yields __________.
amines
19. The Gabriel phthalimide synthesis is used for the preparation of primary __________ without the formation of secondary or tertiary amines.
aliphatic amines
20. Hofmann bromamide degradation reaction involves the treatment of an amide with bromine in an aqueous or alcoholic solution of sodium hydroxide to yield a primary amine with one carbon atom __________ than the original amide.
less
21. Aniline ($C_6H_5NH_2$) undergoes electrophilic substitution reactions on the benzene ring. The $-NH_2$ group is strongly __________ and ortho, para directing.
activating
22. In the nitration of aniline, protection of the amino group by __________ is necessary to control the reaction and obtain the para isomer as the major product.
acetylation
23. Aniline reacts with bromine water to give a white precipitate of 2,4,6-__________ aniline.
tribromo
24. Diazonium salts are very versatile and can be used to synthesize a variety of aromatic compounds through __________ reactions.
replacement or substitution
25. The Hinsberg's test is used to distinguish between primary, secondary, and tertiary amines using benzenesulfonyl chloride ($C_6H_5SO_2Cl$). Primary amines form an N-alkylbenzenesulfonamide that is soluble in alkali. Secondary amines form an N,N-dialkylbenzenesulfonamide that is insoluble in alkali. Tertiary amines do not react.
true
