Coordination Compounds - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

1. Coordination compounds are molecular compounds in which a central metal atom or ion is bonded to a number of __________ or molecules by coordinate bonds.
ligands
2. The central metal atom or ion in a coordination compound acts as a __________ acid.
Lewis
3. Ligands are Lewis __________ that donate one or more electron pairs to the central metal atom or ion.
bases
4. A ligand that binds to the metal ion through a single donor atom is called a __________ ligand.
unidentate
5. A ligand that binds to the metal ion through two donor atoms is called a __________ ligand.
bidentate
6. A ligand that binds to the metal ion through more than two donor atoms is called a __________ ligand.
polydentate
7. When a polydentate ligand uses two or more donor atoms to bind to a single metal ion, it forms a ring-like structure called a __________.
chelate
8. The number of coordinate bonds formed by the central metal atom or ion with the ligands is called its __________ number.
coordination
9. In the IUPAC nomenclature of coordination compounds, the ligands are named in __________ order before the name of the metal.
alphabetical
10. If the complex ion is an anion, the name of the metal ends with the suffix __________.
-ate
11. The oxidation state of the central metal atom in a coordination entity is indicated by a Roman numeral in __________ following the name of the metal.
parentheses
12. Isomers that have the same molecular formula but different arrangements of ligands around the central metal atom are called __________ isomers.
stereoisomers
13. Geometrical isomerism arises in coordination compounds due to different possible geometric arrangements of ligands around the central metal atom. It is common in __________ and octahedral complexes.
square planar
14. Optical isomerism arises in chiral molecules that are non-superimposable on their mirror images. Octahedral complexes with certain bidentate ligands can exhibit __________ isomerism.
optical
15. Isomers that have the same molecular formula but differ in the way the ligands are attached to the metal atom are called __________ isomers.
structural
16. Linkage isomerism arises when a __________ ligand can coordinate to the metal atom through different donor atoms.
ambidentate
17. Coordination isomerism occurs in salts containing complex cations and complex anions, where there is an exchange of ligands between the __________ ions.
metal
18. Ionization isomerism occurs when the counter ion in a salt is also a potential __________ and can displace a ligand in the coordination sphere.
ligand
19. Hydrate isomerism is a type of solvate isomerism where water molecules are involved as __________ within the coordination sphere or as water of crystallization.
ligands
20. Valence Bond Theory (VBT) explains the bonding in coordination compounds in terms of hybridization of metal orbitals and overlap with ligand orbitals to form __________ bonds.
coordinate
21. Crystal Field Theory (CFT) considers the interaction between the metal ion and the ligands as purely __________ in nature.
electrostatic
22. In an octahedral crystal field, the d-orbitals of the central metal ion split into two sets: $t_{2g}$ (lower energy) and $e_g$ (higher energy). The energy difference between these two sets is denoted by $\Delta_o$ or __________.
CFSE (Crystal Field Splitting Energy)
23. The magnitude of $\Delta_o$ depends on the nature of the ligands. Ligands that cause a large splitting are called __________ field ligands.
strong
24. Ligands that cause a small splitting are called __________ field ligands.
weak
25. The spectrochemical series arranges ligands in the order of their increasing ability to cause splitting of the d-orbitals, i.e., increasing __________.
$\Delta_o$
