Alcohols, Phenols, and Ethers - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz - Phenols

1. Phenols are organic compounds in which the hydroxyl (-OH) group is directly attached to an $sp^2$ hybridized carbon atom of an __________ ring.
aromatic (benzene)
2. The carbon-oxygen bond length in phenol is slightly __________ than that in methanol due to the partial double bond character resulting from resonance.
shorter
3. Phenols are __________ acidic than alcohols due to the stabilization of the phenoxide ion by resonance.
more
4. The acidity of phenols is increased by electron-__________ groups (like $-NO_2$) attached to the benzene ring, especially at ortho and para positions.
withdrawing
5. The acidity of phenols is decreased by electron-__________ groups (like $-CH_3$) attached to the benzene ring.
donating
6. Phenol reacts with sodium hydroxide ($NaOH$) to form sodium __________ and water.
phenoxide
7. Phenol undergoes electrophilic aromatic __________ reactions readily due to the activation of the benzene ring by the hydroxyl group.
substitution
8. The hydroxyl group in phenol is strongly __________ and ortho, para directing in electrophilic substitution reactions.
activating
9. Phenol reacts with bromine water to give a white precipitate of 2,4,6-tribromophenol. This reaction is used as a test for the presence of a __________ group.
phenolic (-OH attached to benzene ring)
10. Nitration of phenol with dilute nitric acid at low temperature yields ortho-nitrophenol and __________-nitrophenol.
para
11. Nitration of phenol with concentrated nitric acid in the presence of concentrated sulfuric acid yields 2,4,6-trinitrophenol, also known as __________ acid.
picric
12. Phenol undergoes Friedel-Crafts alkylation and acylation reactions, but the reaction conditions need to be carefully controlled due to the high reactivity of the __________ ring.
benzene
13. Reaction of phenol with zinc dust results in the formation of __________.
benzene
14. Phenol reacts with chloroform in the presence of aqueous sodium hydroxide to form salicylaldehyde. This reaction is known as __________ reaction.
Reimer-Tiemann
15. Phenol reacts with carbon dioxide in the presence of sodium hydroxide under pressure to form salicylic acid. This reaction is known as __________ reaction.
Kolbe-Schmitt
16. Oxidation of phenol with chromic acid or alkaline potassium permanganate under vigorous conditions yields complex mixtures including __________.
benzoquinone
17. Phenol is used as a starting material for the manufacture of Bakelite, a __________.
polymer (thermosetting plastic)
18. Phenol is also used in the production of __________, which are used as disinfectants.
phenolic resins
19. The reaction of phenol with acetyl chloride ($CH_3COCl$) in the presence of anhydrous aluminum chloride yields phenyl __________.
ethanoate (acetate)
20. Ethers can be prepared by the dehydration of alcohols in the presence of concentrated sulfuric acid. Phenols do not readily undergo this reaction to form aryl ethers under similar conditions due to the stability of the __________ ring.
benzene
21. Williamson ether synthesis can be used to prepare aryl ethers by reacting sodium phenoxide with an __________.
alkyl halide
22. The $-\text{OH}$ group in phenols is directly attached to a __________ hybridized carbon atom.
$sp^2$
23. Phenols have __________ boiling points than the corresponding hydrocarbons due to intermolecular hydrogen bonding.
higher
24. Phenols are sparingly soluble in water but dissolve readily in aqueous __________.
alkalies (like NaOH)
25. The reaction of phenol with formaldehyde in the presence of an acid or base catalyst leads to the formation of linear polymers called __________, which are precursors to Bakelite.
novolac
