Diazonium Salts - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

1. Diazonium salts have the general formula $ArN_2^+X^-$, where $Ar$ represents an aryl group and $X^-$ is an anion such as $Cl^−, Br^−, BF_4^−$, etc. The $N_2^+$ group is called the __________ group.
diazonium
2. The process of converting a primary aromatic amine into its diazonium salt is called __________.
diazotization
3. Diazonium salts are typically prepared by reacting a primary aromatic amine with __________ ($NaNO_2 + HCl$) at low temperatures ($0-5^\circ C$).
nitrous acid ($HNO_2$)
4. Aliphatic diazonium salts are highly __________ even at low temperatures and decompose readily to form carbocations.
unstable
5. Aromatic diazonium salts are relatively __________ at low temperatures due to the delocalization of the positive charge of the diazonium ion over the benzene ring through resonance.
stable
6. The reaction of aryl diazonium salts with $CuCl, CuBr,$ or $CuCN$ results in the replacement of the diazonium group by $-Cl, -Br,$ or $-CN$ respectively. This reaction is known as __________ reaction.
Sandmeyer
7. A modification of the Sandmeyer reaction where copper powder is used instead of cuprous salts is called __________ reaction.
Gattermann
8. When aryl diazonium salts are treated with potassium iodide ($KI$), the diazonium group is replaced by __________.
iodine ($-I$)
9. The reaction of aryl diazonium tetrafluoroborate with $NaNO_2$ in the presence of copper on heating leads to the replacement of the diazonium group by __________. This is known as the Balz-Schiemann reaction for preparing aryl fluorides involves $HBF_4$.
nitro group ($-NO_2$)
10. Heating an aqueous solution of diazonium salt leads to the replacement of the diazonium group by __________.
hydroxyl group ($-OH$), forming phenols
11. The diazonium group can be replaced by hydrogen by treating the diazonium salt with reducing agents like hypophosphorous acid ($H_3PO_2$) or __________.
ethanol ($CH_3CH_2OH$)
12. Diazonium salts react with electron-rich aromatic compounds like phenols and amines to form brightly colored azo compounds in a reaction known as __________ reaction.
coupling
13. In coupling reactions with phenols, the reaction is generally carried out in a slightly __________ medium.
alkaline
14. In coupling reactions with amines, the reaction is generally carried out in a slightly __________ medium.
acidic
15. Benzenediazonium chloride reacts with phenol to form p-hydroxyazobenzene, which is an __________ dye.
orange
16. Benzenediazonium chloride reacts with aniline to form p-aminoazobenzene, which is a __________ dye.
yellow
17. Diazonium salts are valuable intermediates in the synthesis of various aromatic compounds, including aryl halides, phenols, nitrobenzenes, and __________.
azo dyes
18. The $-N_2^+$ group is a very good __________ group, which is why it can be easily replaced by various nucleophiles.
leaving
19. Diazonium salts are photosensitive and decompose in the presence of __________ light. This property is utilized in document reproduction.
UV or violet
20. Dry diazonium salts, especially nitrates and perchlorates, are known to be __________. Therefore, they are usually used in solution or as stable salts like tetrafluoroborates.
explosive
21. The reaction of diazonium salts with copper(I) cyanide ($CuCN$) followed by hydrolysis can be used to introduce a __________ group into the aromatic ring, which can then be hydrolyzed to a carboxylic acid.
cyano ($-CN$)
22. The Balz-Schiemann reaction is particularly useful for the preparation of aryl __________, which are often difficult to synthesize by direct halogenation.
fluorides
23. In the structure of diazonium salts, the $C-N \equiv N$ linkage is __________.
linear
24. Diazonium salts can be reduced to hydrazines by treatment with stannous chloride ($SnCl_2$) followed by __________.
alkali
25. Diazonium salts are ionic compounds and are generally __________ in water.
soluble
