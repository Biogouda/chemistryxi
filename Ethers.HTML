Alcohols, Phenols, and Ethers - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz - Ethers

1. Ethers are organic compounds containing an oxygen atom bonded to two __________ groups or aryl groups, or one of each.
alkyl (R)
2. The general formula of aliphatic ethers is $R-O-R'$, where $R$ and $R'$ can be the same or different alkyl groups. They are functional group isomers of __________.
alcohols
3. The $C-O-C$ bond angle in ethers is slightly __________ than the tetrahedral angle ($109.5^\circ$) due to the lone pairs of electrons on the oxygen atom.
greater
4. Ethers have __________ boiling points than the corresponding alcohols due to the absence of intermolecular hydrogen bonding.
lower
5. The solubility of ethers in water is similar to that of alcohols of the same molecular mass because the oxygen atom in ethers can form __________ bonds with water molecules.
hydrogen
6. Ethers are generally __________ reactive than alcohols.
less
7. Ethers do not react with sodium metal, which distinguishes them from __________.
alcohols
8. Ethers undergo cleavage by hydrogen halides ($HX$) to form alkyl halides and __________.
alcohols or alkyl halides
9. The order of reactivity of hydrogen halides towards ethers is $HI > HBr > __________.
HCl
10. Diethyl ether is commonly used as an __________ solvent in the laboratory.
aprotic
11. Williamson __________ synthesis is a common laboratory method for the preparation of ethers.
ether
12. Williamson ether synthesis involves the reaction of an alkyl halide with an __________ of an alcohol or phenol.
alkoxide
13. The reaction of sodium phenoxide with chloromethane yields methyl phenyl __________.
ether (anisole)
14. Symmetrical ethers can be prepared by the acid-catalyzed dehydration of primary alcohols at __________ temperature.
high (e.g., 413 K for ethanol)
15. The acid-catalyzed dehydration of alcohols to form ethers follows an $S_N2$ mechanism when the alcohol is __________.
primary
16. Ethers can undergo electrophilic substitution reactions at the alpha-carbon atoms to the oxygen atom, but these reactions are generally __________ than those of alkenes.
less reactive
17. Cleavage of aryl ethers by $HI$ predominantly yields phenol and alkyl iodide because the $C_{aryl}-O$ bond is stronger than the $C_{alkyl}-O$ bond due to ___________.
resonance
18. Crown ethers are cyclic polyethers that can selectively bind metal ions or small molecules depending on their __________ size.
ring
19. The IUPAC name of diethyl ether is __________.
ethoxyethane
20. The IUPAC name of anisole ($C_6H_5OCH_3$) is methoxy__________.
benzene
21. Ethers can undergo oxidation in the presence of air for prolonged periods to form __________ peroxides, which are explosive.
diethyl ether
22. The oxygen atom in ethers has __________ hybridization.
$sp^3$
23. Ethers are used as __________ in many organic reactions.
solvents
24. Divinyl ether was once used as a general __________.
anesthetic
25. Cyclic ethers containing a three-membered ring are called __________.
epoxides (oxiranes)
