d and f Block Elements - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

1. The d-block elements are also known as __________ metals.
transition
2. The general electronic configuration of d-block elements is $(n-1)d^{1-10} ns^{0-2}$. The (n-1)d orbitals are progressively filled in __________ series.
transition
3. The first transition series starts from scandium (Sc) and ends at __________.
zinc (Zn)
4. Transition metals exhibit variable __________ states due to the participation of both $(n-1)d$ and $ns$ electrons in bonding.
oxidation
5. Many transition metal ions and their compounds are __________ due to the presence of unpaired d-electrons.
paramagnetic
6. Transition metals have a strong tendency to form __________ compounds due to the presence of vacant d-orbitals and their high charge density.
complex
7. Transition metals and their compounds are often used as __________ in many industrial processes.
catalysts
8. The atomic and ionic radii of transition metals generally __________ across a period due to increasing nuclear charge.
decrease
9. The enthalpy of atomization of transition metals is __________ due to strong metallic bonding.
high
10. The highest oxidation state exhibited by manganese (Mn) is __________.
+7
11. Potassium permanganate ($KMnO_4$) is a strong __________ agent.
oxidizing
12. Potassium dichromate ($K_2Cr_2O_7$) is another important oxidizing agent containing chromium in the +__________ oxidation state.
+6
13. The f-block elements are also known as __________ transition metals or inner transition elements.
inner
14. The general electronic configuration of f-block elements is $(n-2)f^{1-14} (n-1)d^{0-1} ns^2$. The (n-2)f orbitals are progressively filled in the __________ and actinoid series.
lanthanoid
15. The lanthanoid series follows lanthanum (La) and consists of 14 elements from cerium (Ce) to __________.
lutetium (Lu)
16. The actinoid series follows actinium (Ac) and consists of 14 elements from thorium (Th) to __________.
lawrencium (Lr)
17. The lanthanoid contraction is the steady decrease in the atomic and ionic radii of lanthanoids with increasing __________.
atomic number
18. The most common oxidation state exhibited by lanthanoids is __________.
+3
19. Many lanthanoid ions are __________ due to the presence of unpaired f-electrons.
colored
20. Actinoids are generally __________ than lanthanoids.
radioactive
21. Actinoids show a greater range of oxidation states compared to lanthanoids due to the smaller energy gap between 5f, 6d, and 7s __________.
orbitals
22. Uranium and thorium are important __________ elements.
actinoid
23. The magnetic moment of transition metal ions can be calculated using the spin-only formula: $\mu = \sqrt{n(n+2)}$ BM, where n is the number of __________ electrons.
unpaired
24. The ability of transition metal ions to form colored compounds is related to __________ transitions of d-electrons.
d-d
25. The catalytic activity of transition metals is attributed to their ability to adopt multiple oxidation states and form __________.
complexes
26. The element with the highest melting point in the first transition series is __________.
chromium (Cr)
27. The only transition metal that is liquid at room temperature is __________.
mercury (Hg)
28. The common oxidation state of zinc (Zn), cadmium (Cd), and mercury (Hg) is __________.
+2
29. The lanthanoid that is used in making colored glasses and as a catalyst is __________.
cerium (Ce)
30. The actinoid that is used as a nuclear fuel is __________.
uranium (U)
31. The tendency of transition metals to form interstitial compounds is due to the presence of small atoms trapped in the __________ of the crystal lattice.
voids
32. Alloys of transition metals are often harder and have higher melting points than the pure metals due to strong __________ bonding.
metallic
33. The lanthanoid oxide used as a catalyst in petroleum cracking is __________.
lanthanum oxide ($La_2O_3$)
34. The actinoid that is used in smoke detectors is __________.
americium (Am)
35. The color of $Cu^{2+}$ ions in aqueous solution is __________.
blue
36. The catalytic converter in automobiles uses transition metals like platinum, palladium, and __________ to catalyze the conversion of harmful gases into less harmful ones.
rhodium (Rh)
37. The lanthanoid that is used in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) contrast agents is __________.
gadolinium (Gd)
38. The actinoid that is the main component of nuclear weapons is __________.
plutonium (Pu)
39. The electronic configuration of $Cr^{2+}$ ion (atomic number of Cr = 24) is __________.
$[Ar] 3d^4$
40. The electronic configuration of $Cu^+$ ion (atomic number of Cu = 29) is __________.
$[Ar] 3d^{10}$
41. The lanthanoid that has an electronic configuration with a half-filled f-subshell is __________.
gadolinium (Gd)
42. The actinoid that has an electronic configuration with a filled f-subshell is __________.
lawrencium (Lr)
43. The magnetic moment of $Fe^{2+}$ ion (atomic number of Fe = 26) is approximately __________ BM.
4.90 (n=4)
44. The color of $Mn^{2+}$ ions in aqueous solution is usually __________.
light pink
45. The lanthanoid that is used in making mischmetal (an alloy used in lighter flints) is __________.
