Carbohydrates - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

1. Carbohydrates are polyhydroxy aldehydes or ketones, or substances that yield these on __________.
hydrolysis
2. Monosaccharides are the simplest carbohydrates that cannot be hydrolyzed further. They are classified based on the number of carbon atoms (e.g., triose, tetrose, pentose, hexose) and the functional group present (aldose or __________).
ketose
3. Glucose is an aldohexose, meaning it has an aldehyde group and __________ carbon atoms.
six
4. Fructose is a ketohexose, meaning it has a ketone group and __________ carbon atoms.
six
5. Monosaccharides exist in solution as cyclic structures formed by intramolecular hemiacetal or hemiketal formation. The carbon atom that forms the new chiral center during cyclization is called the __________ carbon.
anomeric
6. The two cyclic forms of monosaccharides that differ only in the configuration at the anomeric carbon are called __________ and $\beta$ anomers.
$\alpha$
7. Disaccharides are formed by the glycosidic linkage between two __________ units. Examples include sucrose, maltose, and lactose.
monosaccharide
8. Sucrose is a non-reducing sugar composed of glucose and __________ linked by a $\alpha, \beta(1 \rightarrow 2)$ glycosidic bond.
fructose
9. Maltose is a reducing sugar composed of two glucose units linked by a $\alpha(1 \rightarrow 4)$ glycosidic bond. The anomeric carbon of the second glucose unit is __________.
free
10. Lactose is a reducing sugar composed of galactose and glucose linked by a $\beta(1 \rightarrow 4)$ glycosidic bond. It is commonly found in __________.
milk
11. Polysaccharides are long chains of monosaccharide units joined by glycosidic linkages. They serve as storage molecules (e.g., starch and glycogen) or structural materials (e.g., cellulose and __________).
chitin
12. Starch is a polymer of $\alpha$-glucose and consists of two components: amylose (linear chain with $\alpha(1 \rightarrow 4)$ linkages) and amylopectin (branched chain with $\alpha(1 \rightarrow 4)$ and $\alpha(1 \rightarrow 6)$ linkages). It is the main storage polysaccharide in __________.
plants
13. Glycogen is the storage polysaccharide in animals and is structurally similar to amylopectin but more extensively __________.
branched
14. Cellulose is a linear polymer of $\beta$-glucose units linked by $\beta(1 \rightarrow 4)$ glycosidic bonds. It is the main structural component of plant cell __________.
walls
15. Carbohydrates that reduce Tollens' reagent or Fehling's solution are called __________ sugars because they have a free aldehyde or ketone group in their open-chain form or can generate one in solution.
reducing
16. All monosaccharides are __________ sugars.
reducing
17. The reaction of glucose with Tollens' reagent produces a silver mirror, indicating the presence of an __________ group.
aldehyde
18. The reaction of glucose with Fehling's solution produces a red precipitate of cuprous oxide ($Cu_2O$), also confirming the presence of an aldehyde group.
true
19. The oxidation of glucose with nitric acid yields saccharic acid, in which both the aldehyde and the terminal alcohol groups are oxidized to __________ groups.
carboxylic acid
20. The oxidation of glucose with bromine water yields gluconic acid, in which only the __________ group is oxidized to a carboxylic acid.
aldehyde
21. Reduction of glucose with sodium borohydride ($NaBH_4$) or catalytic hydrogenation yields sorbitol (glucitol), a polyhydroxy __________.
alcohol
22. The conversion of glucose to its cyclic hemiacetal form involves the reaction between the aldehyde group at C-1 and the hydroxyl group at C-__________.
5
23. The Haworth projection is a common way to represent the cyclic structures of monosaccharides. In the $\alpha$ anomer of D-glucose, the $-\text{OH}$ group at the anomeric carbon (C-1) is directed __________.
down
24. In the $\beta$ anomer of D-glucose, the $-\text{OH}$ group at the anomeric carbon (C-1) is directed __________.
up
25. Carbohydrates play essential roles in living organisms as energy sources, energy storage, and structural components. They are also involved in cell recognition and other biological processes.
true
