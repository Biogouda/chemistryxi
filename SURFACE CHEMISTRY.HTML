Surface Chemistry - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

1. Surface chemistry deals with phenomena that occur at the __________ between two phases.
interfaces
2. The accumulation of molecular species at the surface rather than in the bulk of a solid or liquid is known as __________.
adsorption
3. The substance on whose surface adsorption occurs is called the __________.
adsorbent
4. The molecular species that gets adsorbed on the surface is called the __________.
adsorbate
5. The process of removing an adsorbed substance from the surface is called __________.
desorption
6. Adsorption in which the adsorbate is held to the surface by weak van der Waals forces is called __________.
physisorption or physical adsorption
7. Adsorption in which the adsorbate is held to the surface by strong chemical bonds is called __________.
chemisorption or chemical adsorption
8. Physisorption is generally __________ in nature.
reversible
9. Chemisorption is generally __________ in nature.
irreversible
10. The extent of physisorption decreases with an increase in __________.
temperature
11. The extent of adsorption of a gas on a solid surface is given by the adsorption __________.
isotherm
12. The Freundlich adsorption isotherm gives an empirical relationship between the quantity of gas adsorbed by unit mass of solid adsorbent and __________ at a particular temperature.
pressure
13. The Langmuir adsorption isotherm is based on the assumption that adsorption occurs in the form of a __________ layer.
monolayer
14. A __________ is a colloidal solution in which the dispersed phase is a liquid and the dispersion medium is a solid.
gel
15. Colloidal solutions are heterogeneous systems in which the particle size ranges from approximately __________ to __________ nanometers.
1, 1000
16. Colloidal solutions in which the dispersed phase and dispersion medium have strong affinity for each other are called __________ colloids.
lyophilic
17. Colloidal solutions in which the dispersed phase and dispersion medium have little affinity for each other are called __________ colloids.
lyophobic
18. The scattering of light by colloidal particles is known as the __________ effect.
Tyndall
19. The zigzag movement of colloidal particles in a dispersion medium is called __________ movement.
Brownian
20. The migration of colloidal particles under the influence of an electric field is called __________.
electrophoresis
21. The coagulation or precipitation of lyophobic colloids can be brought about by the addition of __________.
electrolytes
22. The minimum concentration of an electrolyte required to cause coagulation of a colloidal solution in two hours is called its __________ value.
coagulating or flocculating
23. Hardy-Schulze rule states that the coagulating power of an electrolyte is directly proportional to the __________ of the ions carrying the charge opposite to that of the colloidal particles.
valency
24. Lyophilic colloids are more stable than lyophobic colloids because they are extensively __________.
hydrated or solvated
25. The process of protecting a lyophobic colloid by the addition of a lyophilic colloid is called __________.
protection of colloids
26. The number of milligrams of a lyophilic colloid required to prevent the coagulation of 10 mL of a standard gold sol by the addition of 1 mL of a 10% NaCl solution is called its __________ number.
gold
27. Emulsions are colloidal solutions in which both the dispersed phase and the dispersion medium are __________.
liquids
28. An emulsion of oil in water (O/W) has __________ as the dispersed phase and __________ as the dispersion medium.
oil, water
29. An emulsion of water in oil (W/O) has __________ as the dispersed phase and __________ as the dispersion medium.
water, oil
30. Emulsions are generally unstable and tend to separate into two liquid layers. To stabilize them, an __________ is usually added.
emulsifier or emulsifying agent
