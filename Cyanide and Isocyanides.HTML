Cyanides and Isocyanides - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

Cyanides (Nitriles)

1. Cyanides are organic compounds containing the __________ functional group ($−C≡N$).
nitrile (cyano)
2. The carbon and nitrogen atoms in the nitrile group are __________ hybridized, resulting in a linear geometry.
$sp$
3. The IUPAC nomenclature of aliphatic nitriles involves adding the suffix '-__________' to the name of the corresponding alkane. The carbon of the nitrile group is numbered as 1.
nitrile
4. Cyanides have __________ boiling points than the corresponding alkyl halides, alcohols, and amines of similar molecular mass due to strong dipole-dipole interactions.
higher
5. Lower nitriles are soluble in water due to the ability of the nitrogen atom to form __________ bonds with water molecules.
hydrogen
6. Hydrolysis of nitriles in acidic or basic medium yields __________. Complete hydrolysis gives carboxylic acids, while partial hydrolysis gives amides.
carboxylic acids or amides
7. Reduction of nitriles with lithium aluminum hydride ($LiAlH_4$) or catalytic hydrogenation ($H_2/Ni$) yields primary __________.
amines
8. Reaction of nitriles with Grignard reagents ($RMgX$) followed by hydrolysis yields __________.
ketones
9. The Stephen reaction involves the reduction of nitriles with stannous chloride ($SnCl_2$) and $HCl$ followed by hydrolysis to give __________.
aldehydes
10. Cyanide ion ($CN^−$) is an __________ nucleophile as it has two nucleophilic centers (carbon and nitrogen).
ambident
11. Reaction of alkyl halides with $KCN$ (ionic) predominantly yields __________.
alkyl cyanides (nitriles)
12. Reaction of alkyl halides with $AgCN$ (covalent) predominantly yields __________.
alkyl isocyanides (isonitriles)

Isocyanides (Isonitriles or Carbylamines)

13. Isocyanides are organic compounds containing the __________ functional group ($−N^+≡C^−$).
isocyanide (isonitrile or carbylamine)
14. The carbon and nitrogen atoms in the isocyanide group are also __________ hybridized, resulting in a linear arrangement.
$sp$
15. Isocyanides have __________ boiling points than the corresponding nitriles due to weaker dipole-dipole interactions.
lower
16. Isocyanides have a very __________ odor.
unpleasant (foul-smelling)
17. Hydrolysis of isocyanides in acidic medium yields primary amines and __________ acid.
formic ($HCOOH$)
18. Reduction of isocyanides with lithium aluminum hydride ($LiAlH_4$) yields secondary amines with a methyl group attached to the nitrogen atom.
true
19. Isocyanides undergo addition reactions with halogens and sulfur. For example, reaction with chlorine gives alkyl __________ isocyanide.
iminochlorides
20. The carbylamine reaction (isocyanide test) involves the reaction of primary amines with chloroform and alcoholic $KOH$ to form __________.
isocyanides
21. Isocyanides can act as __________ in coordination chemistry due to the lone pair of electrons on the carbon atom.
ligands
22. The carbon atom of the isocyanide group carries a __________ formal charge, and the nitrogen atom carries a __________ formal charge.
negative; positive
23. Isocyanides are also known as __________.
isonitriles or carbylamines
24. Unlike nitriles, isocyanides are generally considered to be __________ stable.
less
25. The carbon-nitrogen bond in isocyanides is a triple bond ($N^+≡C^−$) where the nitrogen donates a lone pair to carbon, resulting in a coordinate covalent bond.
false (it's a coordinate covalent bond from carbon to nitrogen in the resonance hybrid)
