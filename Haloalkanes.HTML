Haloalkanes and Haloarenes - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

Haloalkanes

1. Haloalkanes are organic compounds in which one or more hydrogen atoms of an alkane have been replaced by __________ atoms.
halogen
2. The general formula of monohaloalkanes is $C_nH_{2n+1}X$, where X represents a __________.
halogen atom (F, Cl, Br, I)
3. Haloalkanes are also known as alkyl __________.
halides
4. The carbon-halogen bond in haloalkanes is __________ due to the difference in electronegativity between carbon and halogen.
polar
5. The boiling points of haloalkanes are considerably __________ than those of the corresponding alkanes due to stronger dipole-dipole interactions and higher molecular mass.
higher
6. The density of haloalkanes generally __________ with increasing molecular mass and number of halogen atoms.
increases
7. Haloalkanes undergo nucleophilic __________ reactions because the carbon atom bonded to the halogen is electron-deficient.
substitution
8. $S_N1$ reactions proceed through the formation of a __________.
carbocation
9. $S_N2$ reactions are bimolecular and proceed through a __________ state.
transition
10. The reactivity of haloalkanes in $S_N1$ reactions follows the order: tertiary > secondary > __________.
primary
11. The reactivity of haloalkanes in $S_N2$ reactions follows the order: primary > secondary > __________.
tertiary
12. Haloalkanes undergo elimination reactions ($β$-elimination) in the presence of a strong base to form __________.
alkenes
13. In dehydrohalogenation, a hydrogen atom from the β-carbon and a halogen atom from the α-carbon are removed. This reaction is also known as a __________ reaction.
β-elimination
14. The reaction of haloalkanes with magnesium in dry ether gives alkylmagnesium halides, which are known as __________ reagents.
Grignard
15. Grignard reagents are very reactive towards compounds containing __________ bonds.
polar multiple (e.g., C=O, C≡N)

Haloarenes

16. Haloarenes are organic compounds in which one or more hydrogen atoms of an aromatic ring have been replaced by __________ atoms.
halogen
17. The carbon-halogen bond in haloarenes has some __________ bond character due to resonance.
double
18. Haloarenes are generally __________ reactive towards nucleophilic substitution reactions compared to haloalkanes.
less
19. The presence of electron-__________ groups at ortho and para positions to the halogen in haloarenes facilitates nucleophilic substitution reactions.
withdrawing
20. The reaction of chlorobenzene with sodium hydroxide at high temperature and pressure to form phenol is known as __________ process.
Dow's
21. Haloarenes undergo electrophilic __________ reactions on the aromatic ring.
substitution
22. The halogen atom in haloarenes is __________ directing in electrophilic substitution reactions.
ortho- and para-
23. Freon-12 ($CCl_2F_2$) is a common __________ used in refrigeration and air conditioning.
refrigerant
24. DDT (dichlorodiphenyltrichloroethane) is a well-known organochlorine __________ that was widely used in the past.
insecticide
25. Polyhalogen compounds like carbon tetrachloride ($CCl_4$) are used as solvents and in the production of __________.
refrigerants
