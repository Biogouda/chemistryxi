Electrochemistry - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

1. The branch of chemistry that deals with the relationship between electrical energy and chemical reactions is called __________.
electrochemistry
2. A device that converts chemical energy into electrical energy is called a __________ cell or voltaic cell.
galvanic
3. A device that uses electrical energy to carry out non-spontaneous chemical reactions is called an __________ cell.
electrolytic
4. Oxidation is the __________ of electrons, and reduction is the __________ of electrons.
loss, gain
5. In a galvanic cell, oxidation occurs at the __________ (negative electrode), and reduction occurs at the __________ (positive electrode).
anode, cathode
6. In an electrolytic cell, oxidation occurs at the __________ (positive electrode), and reduction occurs at the __________ (negative electrode).
anode, cathode
7. The difference in electric potential between the two electrodes of a galvanic cell is called the cell potential or __________.
electromotive force (EMF)
8. The standard electrode potential ($E^\ominus$) is the potential of an electrode measured under standard conditions: 298 K, 1 atm pressure for gases, and __________ M concentration for solutions.
1
9. The standard hydrogen electrode (SHE) has an arbitrarily assigned standard electrode potential of __________ volts.
0.00
10. The cell potential ($E_{cell}$) can be calculated using the standard electrode potentials of the cathode and anode as $E_{cell}^\ominus = E_{cathode}^\ominus - E_{anode}^\ominus$. Here, both potentials are __________ potentials.
reduction
11. The Nernst equation relates the electrode potential to the standard electrode potential and the __________ of the species involved in the electrochemical reaction.
concentrations
12. For a general reaction $aA + bB \rightarrow cC + dD$, the Nernst equation for the cell potential is $E_{cell} = E_{cell}^\ominus - \frac{RT}{nF} \ln Q$, where Q is the __________.
reaction quotient
13. At equilibrium, the cell potential ($E_{cell}$) is __________, and the reaction quotient (Q) is equal to the equilibrium constant ($K_c$).
zero
14. The relationship between the standard cell potential ($E_{cell}^\ominus$) and the equilibrium constant ($K_c$) is given by $\ln K_c = \frac{nFE_{cell}^\ominus}{RT}$. Here, R is the gas constant, T is the temperature, n is the number of moles of electrons transferred, and F is the __________ constant.
Faraday
15. The amount of substance deposited or liberated at an electrode during electrolysis is directly proportional to the quantity of __________ passed through the electrolytic solution (Faraday's first law).
electricity
16. One Faraday (F) is equal to the charge of one mole of electrons, which is approximately __________ Coulombs.
96500
17. The mass of a substance deposited or liberated at an electrode by one Coulomb of charge is called its __________.
electrochemical equivalent
18. Primary batteries are those that cannot be __________ after they have been used.
recharged
19. Secondary batteries are those that can be __________ by passing current through them in the opposite direction.
recharged
20. The lead storage battery is a common example of a __________ battery.
secondary
21. Fuel cells are galvanic cells that are designed to continuously convert the chemical energy of a fuel like hydrogen and an oxidant like oxygen into __________.
electrical energy
22. Corrosion is the process of slow deterioration of metals due to their reaction with air and __________.
water
23. Rusting of iron involves the formation of hydrated iron(III) oxide, whose chemical formula is typically represented as $Fe_2O_3 \cdot xH_2O$. This is an example of __________.
corrosion
24. One method to prevent corrosion is by coating the metal surface with paint or __________.
grease
25. Galvanization is a method of protecting iron from rusting by coating it with a layer of __________.
zinc
