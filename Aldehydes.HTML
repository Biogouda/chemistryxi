Aldehydes, Ketones, and Carboxylic Acids - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz - Aldehydes

1. Aldehydes are organic compounds containing a carbonyl group ($C=O$) with the carbon atom bonded to at least one __________ atom and one alkyl or aryl group.
hydrogen
2. The general formula of aliphatic aldehydes is $RCHO$, and the carbonyl group is always at the __________ of the carbon chain.
end
3. The IUPAC nomenclature of aldehydes involves replacing the '-e' of the corresponding alkane with '-__________'.
al
4. The carbon atom of the carbonyl group in aldehydes is __________ hybridized and has a trigonal planar geometry.
$sp^2$
5. Aldehydes have __________ boiling points than the corresponding alkanes but __________ boiling points than the corresponding alcohols due to the presence of polar carbonyl group but absence of strong hydrogen bonding.
higher; lower
6. Lower aldehydes are soluble in water due to the formation of hydrogen bonds with water molecules through the __________ oxygen atom.
carbonyl
7. Aldehydes undergo nucleophilic __________ reactions at the electrophilic carbonyl carbon.
addition
8. Aldehydes react with alcohols in the presence of dry $HCl$ to form hemiacetals and further reaction yields __________.
acetals
9. Aldehydes react with ammonia derivatives (like hydroxylamine, hydrazine, phenylhydrazine) to form condensation products such as oximes, hydrazones, and __________.
phenylhydrazones
10. Aldehydes can be reduced to primary alcohols using reducing agents like $LiAlH_4$ or $NaBH_4$. This reduction involves the addition of __________ across the carbonyl double bond.
hydrogen
11. Oxidation of aldehydes with common oxidizing agents like $KMnO_4$ or $K_2Cr_2O_7$ yields __________.
carboxylic acids
12. Aldehydes can be distinguished from ketones using __________ reagent, which aldehydes reduce to a silver mirror.
Tollens'
13. Aldehydes also reduce Fehling's solution (a complex of $Cu^{2+}$ ions) to a red precipitate of cuprous oxide ($Cu_2O$). This is another test to distinguish aldehydes from __________.
ketones
14. Aldehydes undergo aldol condensation in the presence of dilute alkali to form $\beta$-hydroxy aldehydes (aldols). This reaction involves the formation of an __________ ion.
enolate
15. Aldehydes that do not have an $\alpha$-hydrogen atom undergo Cannizzaro reaction in the presence of concentrated alkali to give a primary alcohol and a salt of a __________ acid.
carboxylic
16. Formaldehyde ($HCHO$) is the simplest aldehyde and is used in the manufacture of __________ and other polymers.
Bakelite
17. Acetaldehyde ($CH_3CHO$) is used in the production of acetic acid and __________.
ethanol
18. Aromatic aldehydes, like benzaldehyde ($C_6H_5CHO$), undergo electrophilic substitution reactions on the benzene ring. The $-CHO$ group is __________ and meta directing.
deactivating
19. The oxidation of primary alcohols with pyridinium chlorochromate (PCC) stops at the __________ stage.
aldehyde
20. Rosenmund reduction involves the hydrogenation of acyl chlorides over palladium on barium sulfate ($Pd/BaSO_4$) to yield __________.
aldehydes
21. The Stephen reaction involves the reduction of nitriles with stannous chloride ($SnCl_2$) and $HCl$ followed by hydrolysis to give __________.
aldehydes
22. The Etard reaction involves the oxidation of toluene with chromyl chloride ($CrO_2Cl_2$) to yield __________.
benzaldehyde
23. Aldehydes undergo Wittig reaction with phosphorus ylides (phosphoranes) to form __________.
alkenes
24. The Clemmensen reduction involves the reduction of aldehydes and ketones to alkanes using zinc amalgam ($Zn-Hg$) and concentrated __________.
HCl
25. The Wolff-Kishner reduction involves the reduction of aldehydes and ketones to alkanes using hydrazine ($NH_2NH_2$) followed by heating with $KOH$ in ethylene glycol. This reaction proceeds via the formation of a __________.
hydrazone
