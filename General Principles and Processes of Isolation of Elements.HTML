General Principles and Processes of Isolation of Elements - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

1. The naturally occurring chemical substances in the earth's crust containing metals in combined state are called __________.
minerals
2. The minerals from which metals can be extracted conveniently and economically are called __________.
ores
3. The unwanted impurities present in an ore are called __________.
gangue or matrix
4. The process of separating the ore from the gangue is called concentration or __________.
ore dressing
5. A method of ore concentration based on the difference in densities of the ore and the gangue particles is called __________ separation.
gravity
6. The process of separating sulfide ores by preferential wetting with oil is called __________ flotation.
froth
7. Substances added to the froth flotation process to selectively prevent certain ores from forming froth are called __________.
depressants
8. This method of ore concentration is based on the difference in magnetic properties of the ore and the gangue.
magnetic separation
9. In this method of ore concentration, the ore is dissolved in a suitable solvent, and the impurities are left undissolved. The metal is then recovered by precipitation or electrolysis. This is called __________.
leaching
10. Roasting is a process of heating the concentrated ore in the presence of excess __________.
air
11. Calcination is a process of heating the concentrated ore in the absence or limited supply of __________.
air
12. Smelting involves heating the roasted or calcined ore with a reducing agent like carbon or __________ in a furnace.
coke
13. A substance added during smelting to remove the gangue that is not removed during concentration is called __________.
flux
14. The fusible product formed when the flux reacts with the gangue is called __________.
slag
15. Extraction of metals high up in the reactivity series is usually carried out by __________ of their molten salts.
electrolysis
16. Mond's process is used for the refining of __________.
nickel
17. Van Arkel method is used for obtaining ultra-pure __________ and zirconium.
titanium
18. The process of purifying a metal by heating it in a stream of its own volatile compound, which then decomposes to give the pure metal, is called __________ refining.
vapor phase
19. Liquation is a method for refining metals with __________ melting points than their impurities.
low
20. Poling is a method used for the refining of metals like copper and tin, where the molten metal is stirred with green wood poles to reduce the metal __________.
oxides
21. Electrolytic refining is used to obtain metals of high purity, such as copper, silver, and __________.
gold

23. In the blast furnace used for the extraction of iron, the reducing agent is __________.
carbon monoxide (CO)
24. The slag formed in the blast furnace during iron extraction is mainly __________.
calcium silicate ($CaSiO_3$)
25. Bessemerization is a process used in the extraction of __________ from cast iron.
steel
