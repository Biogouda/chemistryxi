Cleansing Agents: Soap and Detergents

Soaps

1. Soaps are sodium or potassium salts of long-chain __________ acids (fatty acids).
carboxylic
2. Soaps are produced by the saponification process, which involves the hydrolysis of fats and oils with an __________.
alkali (like NaOH or KOH)
3. A soap molecule has two parts: a long hydrocarbon chain that is __________ (water-repelling) and an ionic carboxylate head that is __________ (water-attracting).
hydrophobic; hydrophilic
4. Soaps work as emulsifying agents because the hydrophobic tail dissolves in oil and grease, while the hydrophilic head interacts with __________.
water
5. Soaps form __________ in water, which are spherical clusters of soap molecules with the hydrophobic tails pointing inwards towards the oil/dirt and the hydrophilic heads pointing outwards towards the water.
micelles
6. Soaps are generally effective in __________ water but form scum (an insoluble precipitate) in hard water due to the presence of calcium and magnesium ions.
soft

Detergents

7. Detergents are synthetic cleansing agents that have properties similar to soaps but do not form scum in __________ water.
hard
8. Anionic detergents are a common type of detergent and include sodium salts of long-chain benzene sulfonic acids or long-chain alkyl hydrogen __________.
sulfates
9. Like soaps, detergent molecules also have a __________ hydrophobic hydrocarbon tail and a hydrophilic ionic head (e.g., sulfonate or sulfate group).
long
10. Cationic detergents are quaternary ammonium salts with a long hydrocarbon chain and a positive charge on the nitrogen atom. They are often used as __________ and fabric softeners.
germicides
11. Non-ionic detergents do not contain any ions and are often esters of long-chain alcohols and polyethylene glycol. They are effective in removing __________ and grease.
oil

Cleansing Action of Soaps and Detergents

12. The cleansing action of both soaps and detergents relies on their ability to lower the __________ tension of water, allowing it to spread and wet surfaces more effectively.
surface
13. When soap or detergent is added to water containing dirt (often oily or greasy), the __________ tails of the molecules dissolve in the dirt.
hydrophobic
14. The hydrophilic heads of the soap or detergent molecules remain attracted to the water molecules at the surface of the __________ formed around the dirt.
micelle
15. Agitation (like scrubbing or stirring) helps to dislodge the micelles containing the dirt from the surface. The dirt is then suspended in the water as a colloidal solution and can be __________ away.
rinsed
16. The negatively charged surfaces of the micelles (in the case of anionic soaps and detergents) prevent them from coming together and precipitating, thus keeping the dirt __________ in the water.
suspended
17. Detergents are considered better cleansing agents than soaps, especially in hard water, because their calcium and magnesium salts are __________ in water and do not form scum.
soluble
