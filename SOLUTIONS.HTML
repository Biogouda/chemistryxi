Solutions - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

1. A solution with a relatively low concentration of solute is called a __________ solution.
dilute
2. The component of a solution that is present in a larger quantity is called the __________.
solvent
3. The component of a solution that is present in a smaller quantity is called the __________.
solute
4. The number of moles of solute dissolved in one liter of solution is called __________.
molarity
5. The number of moles of solute dissolved in one kilogram of solvent is called __________.
molality
6. The ratio of the number of moles of one component to the total number of moles of all components in a solution is called __________.
mole fraction
7. The mass percentage of a component in a solution is given by (mass of the component / total mass of solution) Ã— __________.
100
8. Henry's law relates the solubility of a gas in a liquid to the __________ of the gas above the liquid.
partial pressure
9. Solutions that obey Raoult's law over the entire range of concentration are called __________ solutions.
ideal
10. Solutions that do not obey Raoult's law are called __________ solutions.
non-ideal
11. Non-ideal solutions showing positive deviations from Raoult's law have __________ intermolecular forces compared to the pure components.
weaker
12. Non-ideal solutions showing negative deviations from Raoult's law have __________ intermolecular forces compared to the pure components.
stronger
13. Azeotropes are binary solutions having the same composition in the liquid and __________ phase and boil at a constant temperature.
vapor
14. The colligative properties depend on the __________ of solute particles, not on their nature.
number
15. The four colligative properties are: relative lowering of vapor pressure, elevation of boiling point, depression of freezing point, and __________.
osmotic pressure
16. The relative lowering of vapor pressure of a solution containing a non-volatile solute is equal to the __________ of the solute.
mole fraction
17. The elevation of boiling point ($\Delta T_b$) is directly proportional to the __________ of the solute.
molality
18. The constant of proportionality in the elevation of boiling point equation ($\Delta T_b = K_b m$) is called the ebullioscopic constant or molal elevation constant of the __________.
solvent
19. The depression of freezing point ($\Delta T_f$) is directly proportional to the __________ of the solute.
molality
20. The constant of proportionality in the depression of freezing point equation ($\Delta T_f = K_f m$) is called the cryoscopic constant or molal depression constant of the __________.
solvent
21. Osmosis is the movement of solvent molecules from a region of __________ concentration of solute to a region of __________ concentration of solute through a semipermeable membrane.
lower, higher
22. The pressure required to prevent the flow of solvent into the solution through a semipermeable membrane is called __________ pressure ($\pi$).
osmotic
23. For dilute solutions, the osmotic pressure is given by $\pi = CRT$, where C is the molarity, R is the gas constant, and T is the __________.
absolute temperature
24. Solutions having the same osmotic pressure at a given temperature are called __________ solutions.
isotonic
25. If a solution has a higher osmotic pressure than another, it is called a __________ solution.
hypertonic
26. If a solution has a lower osmotic pressure than another, it is called a __________ solution.
hypotonic
27. The abnormal molar masses are obtained when the solute undergoes __________ or _________ in the solution.
association, dissociation
28. The van't Hoff factor (i) is the ratio of the __________ colligative property to the calculated colligative property assuming no association or dissociation.
observed
29. For a solute that dissociates in solution, the van't Hoff factor (i) is __________ than 1.
greater
30. For a solute that associates in solution, the van't Hoff factor (i) is __________ than 1.
less
