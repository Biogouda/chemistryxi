Group 17 Elements - The p-Block Elements - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

1. The elements belonging to Group 17 of the periodic table are collectively known as __________.
halogens
2. The general electronic configuration of Group 17 elements is $ns^2 np^?$.
5
3. The first element of Group 17 is __________.
fluorine (F)
4. Halogens exist as diatomic molecules ($X_2$) due to their high __________.
reactivity
5. The electronegativity of halogens __________ down the group.
decreases
6. Fluorine is the most __________ element in the periodic table.
electronegative
7. The oxidizing power of halogens __________ down the group.
decreases
8. The boiling and melting points of halogens __________ down the group due to increasing van der Waals forces.
increase
9. Hydrogen halides ($HX$) are __________ soluble in water.
highly
10. The acidic strength of hydrogen halides ($HX$) __________ down the group ($HF < HCl < HBr < HI$).
increases
11. Due to strong hydrogen bonding, __________ has an exceptionally high boiling point among hydrogen halides.
hydrogen fluoride (HF)
12. Chlorine is prepared industrially by __________ process.
Deacon's
13. Chlorine reacts with cold and dilute NaOH to form sodium chloride ($NaCl$) and sodium __________.
hypochlorite ($NaClO$)
14. Chlorine reacts with hot and concentrated NaOH to form sodium chloride ($NaCl$) and sodium __________.
chlorate ($NaClO_3$)
15. Interhalogen compounds are formed when halogens react with each other. Examples include $ClF$, $BrF_3$, and __________.
$IF_7$ (or other interhalogens)
16. The central atom in interhalogen compounds is usually the __________ electronegative halogen.
less
17. Iodine forms several oxoacids. The oxidation state of iodine in iodic acid ($HIO_3$) is __________.
+5
18. The oxidizing power of oxoacids of halogens follows the order: $HClO_4 > HBrO_4 > HIO_4$. This shows that oxidizing power __________ down the group.
decreases
19. The stability of oxoacids of halogens __________ as the oxidation state of the halogen increases.
decreases
20. Fluorine exhibits only a __________ oxidation state in its compounds.
-1
21. Chlorine, bromine, and iodine exhibit positive oxidation states (+1, +3, +5, +7) in their __________ and interhalogen compounds.
oxoacids
22. Bleaching action of chlorine is due to its __________ nature.
oxidizing
23. Iodine is essential for the proper functioning of the __________ gland.
thyroid
24. Silver bromide ($AgBr$) is used in __________ photography.
black and white
25. The halogen with the highest ionization enthalpy is __________.
fluorine (F)
