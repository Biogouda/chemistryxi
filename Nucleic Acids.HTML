Nucleic Acids: DNA and RNA - Chemistry Class 12 Quiz

Deoxyribonucleic Acid (DNA)

1. DNA is the genetic material in most organisms and contains the instructions for the synthesis of __________.
proteins
2. The building blocks of DNA are __________, each consisting of a deoxyribose sugar, a phosphate group, and one of four nitrogenous bases: adenine (A), guanine (G), cytosine (C), and thymine (T).
deoxyribonucleotides
3. In DNA, adenine (A) always pairs with __________ (T) through two hydrogen bonds, and guanine (G) always pairs with __________ (C) through three hydrogen bonds. This is known as Chargaff's rules.
thymine; cytosine
4. DNA exists as a double helix, with two polynucleotide strands wound around each other. The sugar-phosphate backbone is on the outside, and the nitrogenous bases are on the __________.
inside
5. The two strands of the DNA double helix are __________, meaning they run in opposite directions (5' to 3' and 3' to 5').
antiparallel
6. The process by which DNA makes copies of itself is called __________.
replication
7. The sequence of bases in DNA carries the genetic information. A sequence of three bases (a codon) typically codes for a specific __________.
amino acid
8. The sugar in DNA is __________, which lacks an oxygen atom at the 2' carbon compared to ribose.
2'-deoxyribose
9. DNA is primarily found in the __________ of eukaryotic cells.
nucleus
10. The stability of the DNA double helix is due to hydrogen bonds between the bases and __________ interactions between the stacked bases.
base-stacking (hydrophobic)

Ribonucleic Acid (RNA)

11. RNA is involved in protein synthesis and gene regulation. Unlike DNA, RNA is usually __________-stranded.
single
12. The sugar in RNA is __________.
ribose
13. RNA also contains four nitrogenous bases: adenine (A), guanine (G), cytosine (C), but instead of thymine (T), it contains __________.
uracil (U)
14. In RNA, adenine (A) pairs with __________ (U) through two hydrogen bonds, and guanine (G) pairs with __________ (C) through three hydrogen bonds.
uracil; cytosine
15. There are several types of RNA, including messenger RNA (mRNA), transfer RNA (tRNA), and ribosomal RNA (rRNA), each with specific roles in protein __________.
synthesis
16. __________ RNA carries the genetic information from DNA to the ribosomes.
Messenger (mRNA)
17. __________ RNA carries amino acids to the ribosome and helps in the assembly of the polypeptide chain.
Transfer (tRNA)
18. __________ RNA is a major structural and catalytic component of ribosomes, the protein synthesis machinery.
Ribosomal (rRNA)
19. The process of synthesizing RNA from a DNA template is called __________.
transcription
20. RNA is found in the nucleus, cytoplasm, and __________ of eukaryotic cells.
ribosomes

Key Differences Between DNA and RNA

21. DNA contains deoxyribose sugar, while RNA contains __________ sugar.
ribose
22. DNA contains the base thymine (T), while RNA contains the base __________ (U).
uracil
23. DNA is typically double-stranded, while RNA is usually __________-stranded.
single
24. DNA primarily stores genetic information, while RNA is mainly involved in __________ synthesis.
protein
25. DNA is more __________ than RNA due to its double-stranded structure and the presence of deoxyribose sugar.
stable
